STAGES OF DAMAGE

◉ Step 01 — Breathing In Smoke

THE SMOKE GOES IN

When you smoke, the smoke goes into your lungs. The smoke has more than 7,000 chemicals in it. Your lungs get red and hurt right away. They make extra liquid (mucus) to try to push the chemicals out.

💨 Right away: A gas called carbon monoxide enters your blood. It takes the place of oxygen. Your airways swell up and your body makes 3 times more mucus than normal.

◉ Step 02 — What Happens Soon After

YOUR BODY IS STRESSED

Nicotine — a drug in cigarettes — makes your heart beat faster. Your blood vessels (the tubes that carry blood) get smaller. This means your blood cannot move well. Your blood pressure goes up. You may cough and feel out of breath.

❤️ Heart danger: Each cigarette makes your heart beat too fast for 20–30 minutes. Smokers get heart disease 2 to 4 times more often than non-smokers. — CDC
◉ Step 03 — Tar Builds Up

TAR COATS YOUR LUNGS

Every cigarette puts a sticky black substance called tar inside your lungs. Tar covers the tiny hairs (cilia) in your lungs. These small hairs clean your lungs by pushing out dirt and germs. When tar covers them, they stop working and die.

🚬 Tar fact: A person who smokes one pack a day breathes in about 1 cup of tar every year. The tiny hairs in the lungs die after a few years of smoking.
◉ Step 04 — Long-Term Damage

SERIOUS LUNG DAMAGE

After many years of smoking, the lungs turn black and get smaller. They cannot work well anymore. Bad cells (cancer cells) start to grow. Air pockets form in the lungs — this is called emphysema. It makes breathing very hard.

☣️ Lung cancer: Smoking causes 9 out of 10 lung cancer deaths. Only 18 out of 100 people with lung cancer are still alive after 5 years. Smoking causes 1.8 million cancer deaths every year around the world. — WHO/CDC
◉ Step 05 — If You Stop Smoking

YOUR BODY CAN HEAL

Good news! Your body starts to get better very quickly after you stop smoking. The healing happens step by step. It does not matter how long you have smoked — it is always a good idea to stop.
